Volunteering at Harmony Village

Harmony Village is a facility that houses and cares for those struggling with dementia or Alzheimer’s disease, providing personalized care and helping its residents maintain cognitive function. While we were there, we participated in activities with the seniors such as dancing, reading the daily chronicle, and trivia. Physical activity is important as it delays memory loss, making this daily activity extra important for those at Harmony Village. Additionally, reading and trivia exercise the brain and stimulate its cognitive functions, which is crucial to help those struggling with memory loss as well. We also helped prepare lunch for the seniors, bringing out their meals and making sure they received everything they needed. This was our first volunteering experience, and we are extremely grateful to have had this opportunity to give back to our community. We hope more people can join us at our next volunteering experience!

Alzheimer’s Walk 2024

On Saturday, October 13, 2024, AFA attended the WalktoFight Alzheimer’s event in Paramus at Bergen Community College. Over 500 walkers participated in this event, raising a total of over $150,000 alongside 17 other sponsors. During our time there, we were able to speak with different assisted living homes and connect with various Alzheimer’s Research foundations, which helped us understand what fighting this disease meant to many different people. At the end of the walk, many inspiring speakers, including the mayor of Paterson, took the stage and shared their personal touching stories about losing special loved ones who have succumbed to Alzheimer’s. We were deeply inspired and moved by these stories and how supportive and strong the Alzheimer’s community in Bergen County has become over the years.
